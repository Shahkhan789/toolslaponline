Why compress a PDF?

Large PDFs are slow to upload and may exceed email limits (often 10–25 MB). Compressing a PDF optimizes embedded images, removes redundant data, and streamlines structure so your file stays high-quality yet lightweight.

Step-by-step: compress a PDF using ToolSlap

  1. Go to Compress PDF.
  2. Click “Upload PDF” and select your file.
  3. Choose a compression level (Balanced is recommended for most documents).
  4. Start compression and wait a moment while the file is optimized.
  5. Download your smaller PDF and compare quality if needed.

Best settings for common use-cases

Emails & forms

Use Balanced or Strong compression to meet strict size limits without sacrificing text clarity.

Printing

Choose Light compression to maintain image detail while still cutting redundant data.

Archives

Balanced is ideal for keeping long-term storage small and efficient.

Improve results with simple pre-optimizations

  • Resize large images before embedding. Use our Image Resizer.
  • Convert screenshots to JPG when appropriate to reduce size. Try PNG to JPG or JPG to PNG depending on content.
  • Remove unnecessary pages or combine files efficiently using PDF Merger.

Troubleshooting

  • Text looks fuzzy: Switch to Light compression or re-export the source with higher DPI.
  • File still too large: Downscale large photos first with Image Resizer, then recompress.
  • Form fields lost: Some workflows flatten forms; keep original and test with Balanced mode.
